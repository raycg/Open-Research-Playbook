# Meeting Notes

> **What should I record during a research meeting?**

This template helps researchers prepare for meetings, review progress, and track action items.

Every meeting should **begin by reviewing pending tasks and end with new action items**.

Each action item should be traceable, with a clear owner, expected deliverable, due date, and supporting evidence.

---

## Why?

A research meeting should produce clear decisions and actionable outcomes.

Meeting notes should record only the essential information needed to continue the research.

### 4. New Action Items

A deliverable should be a **verifiable and measurable output**, not a vague activity.

Good deliverables:
- **1** experiment log with **command, configuration, and result**.
- 1 benchmark table comparing at least 2 methods or settings.
- 1 reproduced figure matching the result.
- 1 pull request with a clear description and test result.
- **1** design document explaining the **problem, assumptions, and verification plan**.
- 1 error report with reproduction steps, logs, and environment information.
- 1 updated README section with installation, execution, or troubleshooting steps.
- 1 verification report showing whether the expected result was reproduced.

Avoid vague activities:
- Study the paper.
- Learn the system.
- Try the code.
- Check the issue.
